When Should You Convert JPG to WebP?

🌐 Website Performance

Images are often 50%+ of a webpage's total size. Converting all JPGs to WebP can cut your image payload by a third — directly improving Core Web Vitals and SEO rankings.

🛒 E-Commerce Product Images

Online stores with hundreds of product photos benefit massively from WebP. Smaller images = faster product pages = higher conversion rates and lower bounce rates.

📝 Blog & Content Sites

WordPress, Ghost, and static site generators all support WebP. Convert your featured images and inline photos to WebP for a faster reading experience.

💾 Storage Optimization

Converting your JPG image library to WebP can reclaim 25-35% of storage space — significant savings for cloud storage costs and backup sizes.

💡 Pro Tips for Better JPG to WebP Conversion

• Start at 0.85 quality — this is the sweet spot where file size drops ~30% with virtually no visible quality loss.

• Photos with gradients and smooth transitions convert especially well to WebP — you'll often see the biggest size savings with these image types.

• Already-optimized JPGs may not shrink as much — WebP excels when the source JPG hasn't been aggressively compressed yet.

• Use WebP alongside a JPG fallback — for maximum compatibility, serve WebP to modern browsers with a <picture> tag fallback to JPG for older browsers.

• Need transparency? WebP supports alpha channels. While JPG doesn't, if you need transparent images, consider our SVG to PNG converter or convert PNGs to WebP.

Can I use WebP images on my WordPress site?

Yes! WordPress has natively supported WebP uploads since version 5.8 (2021). Many caching and performance plugins like WP Rocket and Imagify also include automatic WebP conversion and serving.

What's the difference between converting JPG to WebP and compressing a JPG?

JPG compression reduces file size while staying in JPG format — but the format itself has compression limits. JPG compression is great for quick size reduction. WebP conversion changes the format entirely, typically achieving smaller files than even heavily compressed JPGs can, with better visual quality. For maximum size reduction, convert to WebP.
